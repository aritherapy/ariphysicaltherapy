Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 10, 2026  ·  Last updated: June 10, 2026

ARI Physical Therapy ("ARI Physical Therapy," "we," "us," or "our") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting the personal information you share with us. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect through our website at ariphysicaltherapy.com and through our patient communications, how we use and protect it, and the choices and rights you have, including specific rights under California law.

About your medical information. Health information that identifies you and relates to your care ("protected health information," or PHI) is governed by the federal Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) and California's Confidentiality of Medical Information Act (CMIA), and is described in our separate Notice of Privacy Practices. This Privacy Policy governs information collected through our website and general communications. Where this policy and our Notice of Privacy Practices both apply, the Notice of Privacy Practices controls for your PHI.

1. Information We Collect

Information you provide to us

We collect information you give us directly when you request an appointment, contact us, complete a form, or communicate with our staff by phone, email, or text message. This may include your name, phone number, email address, mailing address, the reason for your visit, your provider or referral information, and any other details you choose to share.

Information collected automatically

When you visit our website, we and our service providers may automatically collect limited technical information such as your IP address, browser type, device information, pages viewed, and the dates and times of your visits. This is collected through cookies and similar technologies (see Section 6).

2. How We Use Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • schedule, confirm, reschedule, and remind you about appointments;
  • respond to your questions, requests, and communications;
  • provide and coordinate your physical therapy care;
  • operate, maintain, secure, and improve our website and services;
  • comply with legal, regulatory, and professional obligations.

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not use it for third-party advertising.

3. SMS / Text Messaging

If you provide your mobile number and consent to receive text messages, we may send you SMS messages relating to your care. Our text messages are transactional and service-related only — for example, appointment reminders, appointment confirmations and scheduling, and patient-care notifications. We do not send marketing or promotional text messages.

Your consent and your mobile number are never shared. We do not share or sell your SMS opt-in, mobile number, or text-message consent with any third parties or affiliates for their marketing purposes. Mobile information is shared only with the messaging providers that operate the texting service on our behalf, solely to deliver messages you have asked to receive.

How our text messaging works:

  • Consent. You provide consent verbally — in person or by phone — before we text you. Consent to receive texts is not a condition of receiving care, and you may decline or withdraw it at any time.
  • Message frequency. Message frequency varies based on your appointments and care.
  • Rates. Message and data rates may apply, depending on your mobile carrier and plan.
  • Opt out. You can opt out at any time by replying STOP to any message. You may also opt out by calling us at (661) 282-8584. After you opt out, we may send one final message confirming that you have been unsubscribed.
  • Help. Reply HELP for assistance, or contact us at (661) 282-8584 or contactus@ariptcare.com.

Text messaging is not a secure or encrypted channel. Standard SMS text messages are not encrypted and could be viewed by others with access to your device or, in rare cases, intercepted. To protect your privacy, we keep text messages general and avoid including detailed clinical or sensitive health information. Please do the same in your replies, and do not send sensitive personal or medical details by text. If you would prefer we not contact you by text, let us know and we will use another method.

Full details of our text-messaging program are described in our SMS Terms of Service.

4. How We Share Information

We share personal information only as needed to provide our services and as permitted or required by law. This includes sharing with:

  • Service providers and business associates who perform functions on our behalf — such as our telephone and text-messaging provider, website hosting, and scheduling tools. Where these providers handle protected health information, we require a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) and appropriate safeguards as required by HIPAA.
  • Your care team, such as referring physicians, where relevant to your treatment.
  • Legal and regulatory authorities, when required by law, subpoena, or to protect rights, safety, or property.

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information.

5. Your Right to Confidential Communications

You have the right to request that we communicate with you about your care in a specific way or at a specific location — for example, by phone instead of text, only at a certain number, or only by mail. We will accommodate reasonable requests. To make a request, contact us using the details in Section 11.

6. Cookies & Analytics

Our website may use cookies and similar technologies to operate the site, remember your preferences, and understand how the site is used so we can improve it. You can control or disable cookies through your browser settings; some features of the site may not function properly if cookies are disabled. We do not use cookies to deliver third-party advertising.

7. California Privacy Rights

Medical information (CMIA)

As a California health care provider, we protect your medical information in accordance with the California Confidentiality of Medical Information Act (CMIA), which in many respects provides stronger protections than federal law. We do not disclose your medical information without your authorization except as permitted or required by law (for example, for your treatment, payment, or health care operations).

Consumer rights (CCPA/CPRA)

Personal information that is protected health information under HIPAA or medical information under CMIA is exempt from the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). For any personal information we collect that is not covered by those laws (such as general website data), and to the extent the CCPA applies to us, California residents may have the right to:

  • know what personal information we collect and how we use it;
  • request access to or deletion of their personal information;
  • opt out of the "sale" or "sharing" of personal information; and
  • not be discriminated against for exercising these rights.

We do not sell or share your personal information as those terms are defined under California law. To make a request or ask a question about your California privacy rights, contact us using the details in Section 11.

8. Data Security

We maintain reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect personal information against unauthorized access, use, alteration, or disclosure. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, however, and we cannot guarantee absolute security. If we become aware of a breach affecting your information, we will notify you as required by applicable law.

9. Children's Privacy

Our website is intended for a general audience and is not directed to children under 13. When we provide care to minor patients, we collect and handle their information through our clinical intake process with the involvement of a parent or legal guardian, consistent with HIPAA and CMIA — not through general website features.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Last updated" date at the top of this page and post the updated policy here. Material changes will be made clear. Your continued use of our website or services after an update means you accept the revised policy.

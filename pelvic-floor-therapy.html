Pelvic Health

Pelvic Floor Physical Therapy in Bakersfield

Discreet, one-on-one care for women — to relieve pain, restore control, and rebuild confidence.
Request a Consultation

Pelvic floor dysfunction is far more common than most people realize — and far more treatable. At ARI Physical Therapy, our licensed physical therapists provide discreet, evidence-based pelvic health care for women, one-on-one and in private — helping you restore comfort, function, and confidence.

Conditions we treat

  • Pelvic floor muscle dysfunction
  • Urinary and bowel incontinence
  • Pelvic organ prolapse
  • Pelvic pain & interstitial cystitis
  • Pre- and postpartum recovery
  • Core weakness & return to activity

What to expect

  • A private one-on-one evaluationYour first visit (about 45–60 minutes) is a relaxed, confidential conversation about your history and goals, followed by a gentle assessment.
  • A personalized treatment planHands-on therapy, targeted exercises, and education — built around your body and your goals, never a one-size-fits-all program.
  • Progress at your paceDiscreet, respectful care every step of the way, with a therapist who listens and adjusts as you improve.

No referral needed to start. In California, you can see a physical therapist directly — for up to 12 visits or 45 days — so there's no need to wait for a doctor's note. Some insurance plans still require a referral for coverage; call us at (661) 282-8584 and we'll help you check.

Pelvic floor therapy FAQs

Do I need a referral for pelvic floor therapy?
No. In California you can see a physical therapist directly — for up to 12 visits or 45 days, whichever comes first — so you can start without a doctor's referral. Some insurance plans still require a referral for coverage, so we recommend checking with your plan, and we're happy to help.
Is pelvic floor therapy private and discreet?
Yes. Pelvic floor therapy at ARI is provided one-on-one with a licensed physical therapist in a private treatment room. Your comfort and dignity come first at every visit.
What happens at the first visit?
Your initial evaluation takes about 45 to 60 minutes. We discuss your history and goals, complete an assessment, and begin your personalized treatment plan.
